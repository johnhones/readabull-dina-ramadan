Marketing Strategy
Step 1 - Add Your Keywords to KDP

If you have not already done so, adding the right keywords to your KDP listing is one of the highest-impact actions you can take. Amazon uses your keywords to decide which searches your book appears in. Confirming all seven fields are fully optimised takes 10 minutes and costs nothing.

✓
Lead with “freedom based business book” in field one
This is your #1 target. It is actively searched, directly relevant, and has moderate competition for the demand level it carries. Make it the very first entry in your KDP keyword list. Then fill all remaining six fields with the supporting terms from the Keywords section of this audit.
✓
Use all 7 keyword fields
KDP gives you 7 keyword fields, each supporting up to 50 characters. Use every one. Think about how your reader searches, not how you would describe your book. Phrases like “how to start a freedom based business” or “escape the 9 to 5 book” are more valuable than broad single words.
✓
What not to do
Do not repeat words already in your title or subtitle. Do not use competitor names, celebrity names, or misleading terms. Amazon has tightened its algorithm and listings with irrelevant or manipulative keywords risk being suppressed. Keep them accurate and specific.

Step 3 - Get Some Ratings and Reviews

Amazon distinguishes between ratings and reviews. A rating is simply a star score with no written comment, while a review includes written feedback alongside the stars. Both count towards your overall star rating and help your book appear credible to buyers, but written reviews carry more weight because they give shoppers context and confidence.

Your book is already being found for your primary keyword. The missing piece is what happens when a buyer lands on your listing and sees zero social proof. This is the most urgent step before you spend anything on ads.

✓
Ask for a rating or review inside your book
Add a short, genuine request at the back of your book asking readers to leave a rating or review if they found it useful. Also add one in the middle of the book as many readers never reach the final pages. Keep it natural and never ask for a 5-star review specifically as Amazon takes a dim view of this and readers find it off-putting.
✓
Aim for 10 to 15 ratings and reviews before promoting
Social proof is the single most important factor in converting browsers into buyers on Amazon. Aim for 10 to 15 combined ratings and reviews before you expect consistent organic sales. Do not launch Amazon Ads until you have at least 5 as a listing without social proof will waste your budget.
✓
Use an ARC service to reach readers before launch
ARC (Advance Review Copy) services such as BookSirens and Hidden Gems connect authors with readers who have opted in to receive free books in exchange for honest reviews. These services are legitimate, widely used, and fully compliant with Amazon’s terms of service. They are one of the most effective ways to build early social proof. Do not pay anyone to leave a positive review and do not use services that guarantee a specific star rating. Honest reviews from genuine readers are the only ones worth having.
✓
Promote to communities where your readers already gather
Freedom lifestyle, entrepreneurship, and career change communities are where Dina’s readers already gather. Facebook groups focused on escaping the 9-to-5, business freedom, and working for yourself are natural homes. LinkedIn is also strong for this audience, particularly among professionals considering leaving employment. Reddit communities including r/financialindependence, r/Entrepreneur, and r/personalfinance collectively reach millions of readers who are actively exploring exactly what this book covers. These are not places to post a link. They are places to contribute genuinely, build credibility, and let the book surface naturally through your presence.

Step 4 - Publish a Paperback Edition

Once you are confident in your listing and have some reviews, publishing a paperback edition is a straightforward next step that opens a second revenue stream with no additional editorial work. In the business self-help category, many buyers actively prefer print. They highlight pages, write notes in margins, and keep books on their desks for ongoing reference.

✓
Price it to earn a meaningful royalty
KDP Print books have a minimum list price based on page count and trim size. The royalty is 60% of the list price minus the printing cost. To clear at least £5 per copy, the book needs to be priced high enough to cover KDP’s printing fee and still leave that margin on top. In a professional niche where buyers expect to pay for specialist content, a higher price point is entirely justified.
✓
Business books are reference books
Self-help and business books in print are regularly gifted, highlighted, and kept on desks for reference. Readers who are serious about changing their career or launching a business often want a physical copy they can annotate. A paperback edition would add a second revenue stream and put the book in front of buyers who actively prefer print.
✓
Print formats perform better in Amazon Ads
Print formats tend to earn higher royalties per sale than ebooks, particularly in the business category where readers expect to pay for professional content. A paperback on the same listing increases your average selling price and gives Amazon more formats to surface in search results.
✓
Both formats appear on the same listing
Once the paperback is live, both formats appear on the same Amazon product page. Buyers choose their preferred format, and you earn from both.

Step 6 - Amazon Advertising

Once you have reviews and confidence in your listing, Amazon Sponsored Products advertising can become one of the most effective and rewarding revenue channels available to a self-published author. The principle is straightforward: you invest a controlled daily budget, and if your campaign is optimised correctly, every pound you spend returns more than a pound in royalties.

Unlike most forms of marketing, Amazon Ads are directly measurable. You can see exactly what you are spending, what is converting, and what your return is in real time. This means you can forecast your sales with a level of precision that most businesses can only dream of.

There is real risk in getting it wrong, so seek support if you are unsure. Start with a budget of £5 to £10 per day, test the data, and only scale what is demonstrably working.

✓
Understand your ACoS
ACoS stands for Advertising Cost of Sale, the percentage of each sale that goes towards your ad spend. If your book earns £5 in royalties and you spend £2 on ads to make that sale, your ACoS is 40%. A target ACoS of 30 to 40% is a solid benchmark for KDP books.
✓
Optimise before you scale
Pause keywords that spend without converting. Lower bids on high-ACoS terms. Increase bids on what is working. Do not increase your budget until your ACoS is consistently within your target range.
✓
Scale what works
Once you find a profitable ACoS that holds consistently, increase your daily budget with confidence. Every pound you put in returns more than a pound in royalties. The ceiling is not your budget. It is the total demand for books in your niche.
